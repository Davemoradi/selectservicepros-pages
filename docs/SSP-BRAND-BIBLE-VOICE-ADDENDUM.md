## Voice and Tone

SSP's visual brand is established. This section establishes the **verbal brand** — how SSP sounds in headlines, body copy, emails, ads, and customer support.

### The voice in one sentence

**SSP sounds like a smart contractor who's been in the trades for 20 years and is finally fed up enough with Angi to build something better.**

### Voice principles

1. **Direct.** Short sentences. No hedging. If we have a position, we state it.
2. **Honest about the enemy.** We name Angi and Thumbtack. We don't pretend to be everyone's friend or a "rising tide that lifts all boats." We're the alternative because the existing options are broken.
3. **Respectful of contractors.** They're skilled tradespeople running real businesses. They're the customer. We don't talk down to them. We don't talk about "empowering" them like we're doing them a favor.
4. **Confident, not arrogant.** We don't claim to be "disruptors" or "the future of home services." We just describe what we built and why.
5. **Specific over general.** "Leads capped at 3" beats "fewer competitors per lead." "$1,200 average ticket" beats "lucrative jobs." Numbers and policies, not adjectives.

---

## What the Voice Is Not

- **Not SaaS-speak.** No "platform," "ecosystem," "leverage," "synergy," "stakeholders," "next-generation," "best-in-class."
- **Not soft marketing language.** No "connecting homeowners with skilled professionals" — that's what Angi says.
- **Not buzzword-driven.** "AI" is a tool we use, not a brand attribute. We say what the AI *does*, not that we have AI.
- **Not afraid of plain language.** "Bullshit" is fine in contractor-facing copy where appropriate. Save it for when it matters.
- **Not corporate.** We're a Texas LLC run by a guy. We sound like one.

---

## Words We Use

- Pro / contractor (not "service provider")
- Job (not "service request")
- Lead (not "opportunity")
- Homeowner (not "customer," "consumer," or "user")
- Quote / bid (not "estimate" unless it's specifically that)
- Crew, shop, business — for the contractor's company

## Words We Don't Use

- Service provider, vendor, freelancer
- Solution, offering, suite
- Disrupt, revolutionize, transform
- Empower, unlock, enable
- Best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge
- Stakeholder, ecosystem, platform (sparingly — "the SSP platform" is okay, but don't overuse)

## Voice Examples

### Bad (Generic Marketing Voice)
> "Select Service Pros is the next-generation platform connecting homeowners with skilled service providers through AI-powered matching technology, empowering contractors to grow their businesses."

### Good (SSP Voice)
> "We send contractors real homeowner conversations, not phone numbers. We cap leads at 3 contractors per job. If a lead is fake, it's free. No annual contract. That's it."

---

### Bad (Soft, Hedged)
> "Many of our contractors find that the SSP experience offers improved lead quality compared to other options."

### Good (SSP Voice)
> "Most of our contractors close 1 in 3 SSP leads. On Angi, it's closer to 1 in 10. That's because we cap at 3 contractors and pre-screen with a real conversation, not a form."

---

### Bad (Corporate Apology)
> "We apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused. Our customer success team will reach out to address your concerns."

### Good (SSP Voice)
> "That lead shouldn't have been sent — wrong category. We refunded it. If this happens again, just flag it in the dashboard and it's free. That's the policy."

## Channel-Specific Notes

- **Homepage (homeowner-facing):** Slightly softer than contractor-facing. Confident but welcoming. The homeowner doesn't know about Angi's contractor problem — they know about *their* problem (the broken AC, the leaking roof). Lead with their problem, not our positioning.
- **Contractor recruitment pages:** Full anti-Angi voice. Contractors know exactly what's broken. We name it.
- **Email (contractors):** Personal, direct, signed by David. Like an email from a real person, not a marketing automation.
- **SMS:** Stripped down, plain, A2P-compliant. No marketing emoji, no fake urgency.
- **Support replies:** Direct, accountable, action-first. State what we'll do, then explain why if needed.
- **Social (if/when we add it):** Same voice. No "engagement bait." No "drop a 🔥 if you agree." Real posts about real contractor and homeowner issues.

## When in Doubt

Read the draft out loud. If it sounds like a marketing intern wrote it, rewrite it. If it sounds like a contractor or a homeowner could have written it, ship it.
